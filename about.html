Our Doctors

Dr. Aneet Hooda

Dr. Aneet Hooda is a Family Physician trained in Australia where she also worked at Rockhampton Hospital in the Emergency Department. She did her MD training in Moscow, Russia. She relocated to Canada in 2020 to be close to her family and practised in Mississauga since then. She enjoys all aspects of General Practice and has a particular interest in working with children (Paediatrics) and has completed a Diploma in Child Health from Sydney University, Australia. She also enjoys Women’s health & Family Planning and is trained in skin cancer medicine. Dr. Hooda has experience working with senior patients and worked for past 2 years at a retirement home in Milton, Ontario. She has been actively working at Covid assessment and treatment clinic since November 2021.

Dr. Hooda is fluent in English, Hindi, Punjabi and Urdu, and can converse in Russian as well.

Dr. Beenish Rashid

Dr. Beenish Rashid is a Family Physician. She obtained her medical degree from Dow Medical College Pakistan. She is a certified Family Medicine Specialist CCFP from The College of Family Physician of Canada. She has over Ten years of experience and worked in Pakistan, UAE (Dubai) and Edmonton, Alberta Canada. Dr. Rashid has special interest in women’s health, prenatal care, chronic disease management, preventive health and to serve older population. She practices evidence-based medicine and follows Guidelines. She has been trained for office procedures like IUD insertions & removals, Cryotherapy for Warts treatment and skin tag removals.

Dr. Rashid is Fluent in English, Urdu and Hindi languages.

Dr. Mariam Zakhary

Dr. Zakhary is a family physician with over 15 years of experience. She finished her medical degree in Egypt and completed the family medicine residency program at Université Laval, Quebec. She has been practicing comprehensive family medicine in Brampton, Ontario since 2004. She is now relocating her practice to Oakville. She is currently accepting new patients as she establishes her practice. She has interests in women’s health and pediatrics and is involved in elderly care as well as community palliative medicine. Dr. Zakhary is also a part-time faculty member with the department of Family Medicine at McMaster University, where she teaches medical students and residents.

She is fluent in Arabic, French and Spanish. In her spare time, Dr. Zakhary serves and mentors adolescents in her church community.
